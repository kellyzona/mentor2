MENTOR2 is a digital design and engineering course developed by the Fab Foundation in partnership with MIT's Center for Bits and Atoms and the Teaching Institute for Excellence in STEM as part of DARPA's manufacturing experimentation and outreach program. This course is based off of Fab Academy, taught by Professor Neil Gershenfeld, director of the Center for Bits Atoms at MIT, and introduces skills in digital design, fabrication, electronics, and programming. The course is organized into three week-long modules: Tools & Techniques, Physical Design, and Mechanical Design, culminating in a self-directed final project during the fourth week.
